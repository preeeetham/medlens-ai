# First Aid Protocols — Burns

## Minor Burns (First-Degree)
Signs: Red skin, mild swelling, pain, no blisters

### Immediate Steps:
1. Cool the burn under cool (not cold) running water for at least 10 minutes
2. Do NOT use ice, butter, or toothpaste on the burn
3. After cooling, apply aloe vera gel or a moisturizing lotion
4. Cover with a sterile non-stick bandage
5. Take over-the-counter pain relief if needed (ibuprofen or acetaminophen)

### When to Seek Medical Help:
- If the burn covers an area larger than 3 inches
- If it's on the face, hands, feet, genitals, or over a joint

## Moderate Burns (Second-Degree)
Signs: Red, blotchy skin, blisters, severe pain, swelling

### Immediate Steps:
1. Cool the burn under cool running water for 15-20 minutes
2. Do NOT pop blisters — they protect against infection
3. After cooling, loosely cover with a sterile bandage
4. Do NOT apply adhesive directly to the burn
5. Elevate the burned area if possible
6. Seek medical attention

### When to Call Emergency Services:
- If the burn is larger than your palm
- If the person is a child, elderly, or has a chronic condition

## Severe Burns (Third-Degree)
Signs: White, brown, or charred skin, may not be painful (nerve damage)

### CALL EMERGENCY SERVICES IMMEDIATELY
1. Do NOT run water over severe burns
2. Do NOT remove clothing stuck to the burn
3. Cover loosely with a clean, cool sheet
4. Check for signs of shock: pale skin, weakness, rapid pulse
5. If shock suspected, lay person flat, elevate legs, keep warm
6. Monitor breathing until help arrives

# First Aid Protocols — Cuts and Lacerations

## Minor Cuts
### Immediate Steps:
1. Wash your hands before treating the wound
2. Stop the bleeding by applying gentle pressure with a clean cloth
3. Clean the wound under clean running water
4. Apply antibiotic ointment if available
5. Cover with a sterile bandage or adhesive strip
6. Change the bandage daily

## Deep or Severe Cuts
### Immediate Steps:
1. Apply firm, direct pressure with a clean cloth — do NOT remove it
2. If blood soaks through, add more cloth on top (don't remove the first one)
3. If possible, elevate the injured area above the heart
4. Call emergency services if bleeding doesn't stop after 10 minutes

### Signs of Serious Laceration:
- Cut is deeper than 1/4 inch
- Edges of the wound can't be pushed together
- Bleeding won't stop with pressure
- Cut is on the face, near a joint, or caused by a dirty/rusty object

# First Aid Protocols — Choking

## Adult or Child (Over 1 Year)
### Signs: Clutching throat, inability to speak/cough/breathe, skin turning blue

### Heimlich Maneuver:
1. Stand behind the person, wrap your arms around their waist
2. Make a fist with one hand, place it just above the belly button
3. Grasp your fist with your other hand
4. Give quick, upward thrusts — press inward and upward
5. Repeat until the object is dislodged or the person can breathe
6. If the person becomes unconscious, begin CPR

## Infant (Under 1 Year)
### Steps:
1. Lay the infant face-down on your forearm, supporting the head
2. Give 5 firm back blows between the shoulder blades using the heel of your hand
3. Turn the infant face-up
4. Give 5 chest thrusts using 2 fingers on the breastbone
5. Repeat back blows and chest thrusts until the object is cleared
6. If infant becomes unconscious, begin infant CPR

# First Aid Protocols — Falls and Fractures

## Suspected Fracture
### Signs: Deformity, swelling, bruising, inability to move, grinding sensation, pain

### Immediate Steps:
1. Do NOT try to straighten a broken bone
2. Immobilize the injury — don't let the person move the area
3. Apply ice wrapped in a cloth (not directly on skin) for 20 minutes
4. If an open fracture (bone visible), cover wound with a clean cloth
5. Check circulation below the injury (pulse, skin color, sensation)
6. Seek immediate medical attention

## Suspected Spinal Injury
### CRITICAL — Do NOT move the person unless they are in immediate danger
1. Call emergency services immediately
2. Keep the person still — do not roll, twist, or reposition
3. Place heavy towels or rolled blankets on both sides of the head/neck
4. If the person must be moved (fire, flood), keep head, neck, and back aligned
5. Monitor breathing — if breathing stops, perform jaw-thrust maneuver (NOT head-tilt)

## Head Injury
### Immediate Steps:
1. If unconscious, call emergency services immediately
2. If conscious, keep the person still and monitor
3. Apply ice to any swelling
4. Watch for danger signs: vomiting, confusion, uneven pupils, clear fluid from nose/ears
5. Do NOT let the person sleep for the first few hours — check on them regularly

# First Aid Protocols — Allergic Reactions

## Mild Allergic Reaction
### Signs: Hives, itching, mild swelling

### Steps:
1. Remove the allergen if possible
2. Take an antihistamine (like diphenhydramine/Benadryl) if available
3. Apply cool compresses to itchy areas
4. Monitor for worsening symptoms

## Anaphylaxis (Severe Allergic Reaction)
### Signs: Difficulty breathing, swelling of face/throat, rapid pulse, dizziness, nausea

### CALL EMERGENCY SERVICES IMMEDIATELY
1. If the person has an EpiPen, help them use it immediately
   - Remove cap, press firmly against outer thigh (can go through clothing)
   - Hold for 10 seconds
2. Have the person lie down with legs elevated (unless breathing is difficult)
3. If breathing is difficult, let them sit up
4. Give a second EpiPen dose after 5-15 minutes if no improvement
5. Monitor breathing and be prepared to perform CPR

# First Aid Protocols — Cardiac Arrest and CPR

## Signs: Person is unresponsive, not breathing or only gasping

### CALL EMERGENCY SERVICES IMMEDIATELY

### CPR Steps for Adults:
1. Place the person on their back on a firm surface
2. Place the heel of one hand on the center of the chest (between the nipples)
3. Place your other hand on top, interlace fingers
4. Push hard and fast — at least 2 inches deep, 100-120 compressions per minute
5. Allow full chest recoil between compressions
6. If trained: give 2 rescue breaths after every 30 compressions
   - Tilt head back, lift chin, pinch nose, breathe into mouth for 1 second
7. Continue until emergency services arrive or the person starts breathing

### Using an AED:
1. Turn on the AED and follow voice prompts
2. Attach pads to bare chest as shown in diagram
3. Clear the person — ensure no one is touching them
4. Press shock button if advised
5. Resume CPR immediately after shock

# First Aid Protocols — Seizures

## Signs: Uncontrolled shaking, loss of consciousness, confusion

### Immediate Steps:
1. Clear the area around the person — move sharp/hard objects away
2. Place something soft under their head
3. Time the seizure
4. Do NOT restrain the person or put anything in their mouth
5. Turn the person on their side (recovery position) when the seizure stops
6. Stay with them until they are fully conscious

### Call Emergency Services If:
- Seizure lasts more than 5 minutes
- Person doesn't regain consciousness
- Person is injured during the seizure
- It's their first seizure
- Person has difficulty breathing after the seizure

# First Aid Protocols — Poisoning

## If Ingested:
1. Call Poison Control immediately (1-800-222-1222 in the US)
2. Do NOT induce vomiting unless told to by Poison Control
3. If the person is unconscious, call 911
4. Save the container/substance for identification

## If Inhaled:
1. Move the person to fresh air immediately
2. Call emergency services
3. If not breathing, begin CPR

## If on Skin:
1. Remove contaminated clothing
2. Rinse the skin with running water for 15-20 minutes
3. Call Poison Control

# First Aid Protocols — Nosebleed

### Steps:
1. Sit upright and lean slightly forward (NOT back)
2. Pinch the soft part of the nose shut
3. Hold for 10-15 minutes without checking
4. Apply a cold compress to the bridge of the nose
5. Do NOT blow the nose for several hours after it stops

### Seek Medical Help If:
- Bleeding doesn't stop after 20 minutes
- Nosebleed follows a head injury
- Bleeding is heavy or the person feels faint

# First Aid Protocols — Heatstroke

## Signs: High body temperature (103°F+), hot/red/dry skin, rapid pulse, confusion, unconsciousness

### CALL EMERGENCY SERVICES — This is life-threatening

### Steps:
1. Move the person to shade or a cool area
2. Cool the person rapidly: immerse in cold water, apply ice packs to neck/armpits/groin
3. Fan the person while wetting their skin
4. Do NOT give fluids if the person is unconscious
5. Monitor body temperature until it drops below 101°F
