# Emergency Response Procedures

## When to Call Emergency Services (911 / Local Number)

### Always Call For:
- Unconscious person
- Difficulty breathing or no breathing
- Severe bleeding that won't stop
- Suspected heart attack (chest pain, left arm pain, shortness of breath, sweating)
- Suspected stroke (face drooping, arm weakness, speech difficulty)
- Severe allergic reaction (anaphylaxis)
- Seizure lasting more than 5 minutes
- Severe burns (third-degree)
- Suspected spinal injury
- Poisoning or overdose
- Drowning

### Information to Provide to Dispatch:
1. Your exact location (address, landmarks, cross streets)
2. Nature of the emergency
3. Number of people injured
4. Conditions of the injured (conscious? breathing?)
5. What first aid is being administered
6. Your name and callback number
7. Any known medical conditions of the injured

## Recovery Position
Used for unconscious but breathing persons:
1. Kneel beside the person
2. Place the arm nearest you at a right angle to their body
3. Bring the far arm across the chest, hold the back of their hand against their cheek
4. Pull the far knee up so the foot is flat on the ground
5. Roll the person toward you onto their side
6. Tilt the head back to keep the airway open
7. Adjust the top leg so the hip and knee are at right angles

## Shock Management
### Signs: Pale/cold/clammy skin, rapid breathing, weakness, nausea, confusion

### Steps:
1. Call emergency services
2. Lay the person flat on their back
3. Elevate the legs about 12 inches (unless head/neck/back injury suspected)
4. Keep the person warm with a blanket
5. Do NOT give food or water
6. Monitor breathing and consciousness
7. If the person vomits, turn to recovery position

## Wound Infection Signs (Monitor After Treatment)
- Increased redness spreading from wound
- Increased swelling
- Warmth around the wound
- Pus or discharge
- Fever
- Red streaks leading from wound toward heart
- Seek medical attention if any of these develop
